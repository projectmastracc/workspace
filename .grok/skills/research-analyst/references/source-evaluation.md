# Source Evaluation

## Evidence hierarchy (general)

1. Systematic review / meta-analysis of RCTs
2. Individual RCT (adequately powered, pre-registered)
3. Cohort / case-control (well-controlled)
4. Case series / case reports
5. Animal / in-vitro / mechanistic
6. Expert opinion, narrative review, commentary
7. Preprint (unpeer-reviewed) — label explicitly
8. Retracted — exclude from support; note if cited in discourse

## Trust downgrade triggers

- Industry funding without independent replication
- Single-lab novelty claim with no external validation
- Outcome switching or HARKing (hypothesizing after results known)
- Small n with post-hoc subgroups
- Surrogate endpoints without validation
- Open-label design for subjective outcomes
- High risk of publication bias (positive-results literature only)

## Trust upgrade triggers

- Pre-registered primary endpoint hit
- Replication by independent group
- Dose-response relationship
- Consistent across study designs
- Large effect size with tight CI in representative sample

## Journal / venue signals

- Indexed, peer-reviewed journal with editorial standards
- Preprint servers (bioRxiv, medRxiv) — useful but unverified
- Predatory journal markers: rapid acceptance, vague peer review claims, fee-only model with no credibility
- Retraction Watch patterns: image duplication, data integrity issues

## Citation hygiene

- Trace claims to primary data, not press releases
- Note when a review cites a review (evidence telephone)
- Prefer full text; abstract-only limits methods appraisal
