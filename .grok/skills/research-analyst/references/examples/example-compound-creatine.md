# Example: Compound profile — Creatine (calibration)

## Compound Identity

Creatine monohydrate — ergogenic amino acid derivative. Lenses: **nutrition** (primary), **performance** (secondary).

## Practical Guidance

| Parameter | Recommendation | Certainty |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Dose (maintenance) | 3–5 g/day | **Established** |
| Loading (optional) | 20 g/day × 5–7 days (4 × 5 g), then maintenance | **Probable** |
| Timing | Any time; post-workout with carbs **Speculative** advantage; consistency matters more | **Probable** |
| Form | Monohydrate — most studied; other forms lack superiority evidence | **Established** |
| Hydration | Increase water intake; monitor cramping if dehydrated | **Probable** |

**Not recommended when:** severe renal impairment (**Established** caution); pediatric use without medical oversight.

## Bro-Science vs Evidence

| Claim | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| "20g forever is better" | **Unknown** / not supported — maintenance 3–5 g matches trial protocols |
| "Creatine causes hair loss" | **Speculative** — single DHT association study; not replicated |
| "Needs cycling" | **Contradicted** — safety data support chronic use |

## Compound Context Summary

Creatine is the best-evidenced ergogenic supplement for strength and lean mass (**Established**). Use 3–5 g/day monohydrate; optional loading accelerates saturation. No cycling required. Monitor hydration; avoid if significant renal disease.
