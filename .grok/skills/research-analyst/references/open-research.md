# Open Research — Transparency & Actionable Reusability

Every `/research` output must be **traceable** and **reusable** for compound contextualization.

## Open sourcing requirements

1. **Cite every pivotal claim** — DOI, PMID, or URL
2. **Label inference** — distinguish direct citation from analyst inference
3. **Expose funding/COI** — never hide industry sponsorship
4. **Tag bro-science** — community claims without literature support get explicit **Unknown** labels
5. **Preserve uncertainty** — do not collapse contested evidence into false consensus

## Actionable reusability

Outputs are designed for users to **contextualize and apply** compounds:

| Deliverable | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| Compound profile | Full framing across applicable lenses |
| Practical Guidance | Evidence-graded dosing, timing, stacks, monitoring |
| Compound Context Summary | One-screen actionable educational frame |
| evidence-matrix.json | Machine-readable claims + recommendations |

## Recommendation traceability

Each recommendation in Practical Guidance must link to:

- A cited source (trial, review, label, guideline), OR
- A labeled inference chain ("inferred from mechanism X + animal data Y — **Speculative**")

## What "open" does not mean

- Recommending without evidence (**Unknown** = no recommendation)
- Presenting bro-science as established
- Hiding harm-reduction requirements for performance compounds
