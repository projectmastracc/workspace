# Funding and Conflict of Interest

Always ask: **who paid for this, and what do they gain if the conclusion is positive?**

## What to extract per key source

- Grant funder (NIH, industry, foundation, none declared)
- Author COI declarations (consulting, equity, speaker fees, patents)
- Sponsor role in design, analysis, publication (ICMJE standard)
- Whether data are fully available for independent audit

## Interpretation weights

| Pattern | Weight adjustment |
|---------|-------------------|
| Industry-funded RCT with prespecified endpoints | Moderate trust; still note sponsor |
| Industry-funded with post-hoc positive finding | Downgrade certainty |
| Key opinion leader with undisclosed ties | Flag prominently; downgrade |
| Government / independent academic funding | Neutral to slight upgrade |
| Advocacy group funding on contested topic | Note ideological/financial alignment |
| No funding declared on expensive trial | Investigate further — omission is not innocence |

## Narrative vs truth

- Sponsored studies can be methodologically sound — funding is a weight, not a veto
- Absence of COI declaration ≠ no COI; state "not declared" honestly
- Ghostwriting / sponsor-controlled manuscripts: downgrade and flag
- When ALL positive evidence is industry-sponsored and independent data conflict, say so explicitly

## Reporting requirement

The briefing **Source Integrity** section must name funders and COI for every pivotal study — or state that information was unavailable.
